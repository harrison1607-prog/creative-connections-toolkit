Event Case Study: PictureEast Student Networking & Panel Event

What we learned from our first cross-university student gathering

Date: 2026.2.21 Location: UCL East Marshgate Audience size: Around 40

B. Why this event

Creative industries are often informal and network-driven, which can make pathways difficult to access without existing connections. Underrepresented students may face additional barriers in finding collaborators, learning the unwritten rules of the industry, and approaching professional spaces with confidence.

This event was designed as a student-led, inclusive gathering to make pathways more visible and make networking less intimidating — through structured introductions, a panel with industry voices, and dedicated time for cross-university connection.

F. What this means

Key takeaways

  • Cross-university demand exists for shared creative spaces where students can meet collaborators.
  • Students prioritise collaboration outcomes (finding people to work with) over passive inspiration.
  • Industry presence can increase confidence and clarity by making pathways feel more legible and attainable.
  • Survey signals can inform structured networking formats (e.g., role-based prompts and targeted introductions).
  • Evidence-based documentation strengthens repeatability for future cohorts and partner institutions.

G. What’s next

Not a one-off event, but the starting point of an ongoing campaign.

Planned follow-up

  • Social media content cycle (highlights, quotes, aspirations board)
  • Follow-up message to attendees
